Project Scope

Literatuer survey

The effective implementation of security practices is crucial to protect sensitive patient information, ensure operational continuity, and safeguard the integrity of critical systems and infrastructure. However, there are deviations from the latest security practices and existing security measures that can expose hospitals to significant risks. These deviations include a preliminary access control system, a simple password policy, insecure storage of secure information using spreadsheets or manual documents, the absence of backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity plans, inadequate antivirus and ransomware protection, outdated operating systems, the lack of security evaluation systems, and the absence of security trainings for employees. Addressing these deviations and adopting robust security measures are essential steps for modern hospitals to mitigate risks, prevent data breaches, and maintain the highest level of security in their operations. The literature survey findings are,

1. Access Control System:A modern hospital should have a robust access control system in place to ensure that only authorized personnel have access to sensitive areas and information. A preliminary access control system may not provide adequate security measures, leading to unauthorized access and potential data breaches.


2. Simple Password Policy: A strong password policy is crucial to prevent unauthorized access to systems and accounts. Using simple passwords or not enforcing password complexity requirements increases the risk of unauthorized access and compromises the security of the hospital's systems and data.

3. Insecure Secure Information Storing (Sheets & Manual): Storing sensitive information in insecure formats such as spreadsheets or manual documents is highly risky. These methods lack proper encryption and access controls, making the data vulnerable to unauthorized access and potential breaches.

4.No Backup/DR/BCP Plans: Hospitals should have comprehensive backup, disaster recovery (DR), and business continuity plans (BCP) to ensure the availability and integrity of critical systems and data. Without these plans in place, the hospital is susceptible to data loss, prolonged system downtime, and an inability to provide essential healthcare services during emergencies.

5. No Antivirus/Ransomware Protection: Hospitals are prime targets for cyberattacks, including ransomware. Without adequate antivirus and ransomware protection measures, the hospital's systems are vulnerable to malware infections, which can disrupt operations, compromise patient data, and lead to significant financial and reputational damage.

6.Outdated OS System: Using outdated operating systems increases the risk of security vulnerabilities. Regular patching and updating of the OS are essential to address known vulnerabilities and protect against potential cyber threats. Operating systems that are not up to date may lack necessary security patches, leaving the hospital's systems exposed to attacks.

7. No Security Evaluation Systems: Regular security evaluations and assessments help identify vulnerabilities, evaluate risks, and implement necessary security measures. Without a proper security evaluation system in place, the hospital may be unaware of existing security gaps and fail to address them effectively.

8. No Security Trainings: Employees should receive ongoing security awareness and training programs to educate them about best practices, potential threats, and their role in safeguarding sensitive data. Without regular security training, employees may be unaware of security risks, leading to inadvertent data breaches or non-compliance with security policies.



Research Problem and Solution

Research Problem

The research problem addressed in this study is the deviations from the latest security practices and existing security measures in modern hospitals. The objective is to identify and analyze the specific security practices that are not aligned with current standards and pose potential risks to patient information, operational continuity, and overall infrastructure security. By investigating these deviations, the research aims to highlight the areas where improvements are needed and propose effective solutions to enhance the security posture of modern hospitals. The study will also explore the potential consequences of these deviations, such as unauthorized access, data breaches, system downtime, and reputational damage. Ultimately, the research seeks to provide insights and recommendations to bridge the gaps in security practices and enable modern hospitals to implement robust measures that safeguard sensitive information, ensure uninterrupted healthcare services, and protect against emerging cyber threats.












Solution

To address the research problem, we propose a comprehensive research solution that integrates various techniques to enable secure and privacy-preserving network traffic monitoring in the cloud using edge devices' network data. The solution encompasses the following key components:

• Deep Learning Model: We will design and develop a deep learning model specifically tailored for analyzing network traffic data in real-time. This model will be trained using a diverse dataset comprising network traffic patterns and malware examples. The model will utilize advanced neural network architectures and algorithms to accurately identify potential malware or anomalous behavior within the network traffic.

• Federated Learning Environment: To ensure privacy preservation, we will establish a federated learning environment where edge devices collaboratively train the deep learning model without exchanging raw data. This federated learning approach enables each edge device to locally train the model using its own network traffic data while maintaining data privacy. Differential privacy techniques will be incorporated to further enhance privacy by adding noise to the gradient updates during the training process. Secure Multi-Party Computation (MPC) via a secure gRPC connection will facilitate the aggregation of encrypted model updates from multiple devices while preserving data confidentiality.

• Privacy-Preserving Techniques: The research solution will incorporate federated differential privacy and adversarial privacy techniques to prevent user data leakage during the model training process. By monitoring for unusual user behavior and evaluating the performance of specific models, we can identify potential privacy breaches and implement robust safeguards to protect sensitive information.

• Data Anonymization: To protect the privacy of individuals and comply with data protection regulations, the research solution will employ advanced data anonymization methods. These methods will ensure that any personally identifiable information (PII) or sensitive data within the network traffic is transformed or removed while preserving the utility of the data for analysis and model training purposes. Machine learning techniques specifically designed for data anonymization will be utilized to ensure the accuracy and effectiveness of the anonymization process.

By implementing this research solution, we aim to develop a secure and privacy-preserving system for monitoring network traffic in the cloud. The solution leverages deep learning models, federated learning, differential privacy, secure MPC, and data anonymization techniques to enable accurate detection of malware and anomalous behavior while preserving the privacy of individual users and preventing data breaches or leakage. This research solution has the potential to significantly enhance the security and privacy of network infrastructures in modern environments.

Methodology


The proposed pest and disease Surveillance system consists of 4 main components. They are; WCLWD and its symptom severity identification CCI Identification and progression level determination Deficiency Identification (Mg deficiency and Leaf Scorch Decline) Water resource identification Crowdsourcing for information sharing. Fig 4 illustrates the overall system diagram of the proposed solution which was intended to provide a smart approach for stakeholders, researchers, and Coconut Development Officers (CDOs) to detect the coconut diseases and pest infestations that may affect the coconut industry. As shown in the diagram, the registered users of the system can capture or upload the images which are suspicious. The images are sent to the Amazon Web Services (AWS) backend server where the flask server is deployed. These images are processed in the flask server by the designed DCNN models for disease identification. If WCLWD is found, the symptom severity will be determined using CNN models. Simultaneously, if CCI is identified, the images will go through the Mask-R-CNN model to determine the progression level while the number of caterpillars are extracted using the YOLOv5 object detection algorithm. Images will be classified using the CNN models of Mg deficiency and LSD at the same time. Once the system identifies that the leaves are infected, then the response will be captured by the crowdsourcing platform. The Google Map will be updated with the real time locations (latitude and longitude) of the infected palms. In Addition, the system will automatically send notifications to the farmers and other stakeholders who are at the risk of infection.
